S'fundo Matu: A short bio

If 20 year old S'fundo could only use one word to describe herself? It would definitely be daring. Hailing all the way from a small town of Empangeni, KwaZulu Natal, but spending most of her childhood interchanging between Gauteng and KwaZulu Natal, change is something that S'fundo is well-acquainted with. This is what has shaped her ability to adapted so easily in any environment that she may find herself in. She knows how to make do with what she has.

S'fundo's friends would describe her as a talkative, empathetic introvert. Her knack for talking to people has made her a lot of friends along the way who find solace in her, but in her perfect world, she is in her room, either practicing a dance routine, or reading a book. In public, you'd find her quietly observing a room, but if you find her in her comfort zone, most likely with her friends, she would probably be the most talkative, and the most energetic person in the said room.

S'fundo is known to dabble in a lot of different hobbies. She plays netball, she sings, she dances, and a hobby that she wants to take on next is learning how to play the keyboard, and how to play the accoustic guitar. An aspiration of hers is to one day become a director, making films that she hopes will shape young black girls' high esteem of themselves, and show them that they too, just like S'fundo learnt in her teen years, can be daring.
